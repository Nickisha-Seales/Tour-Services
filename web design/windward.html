Explore the Windward side of the Main Island

Belmont Lookout

Discover the Breathtaking Belmont Lookout Perched high in the hills of St. Vincent, Belmont Lookout is a hidden gem that rewards travelers with one of the most stunning panoramic views on the island. Imagine standing above a lush green valley, with the sparkling blue Caribbean Sea in the distance and the vibrant capital of Kingstown peeking through the hills. Whether you’re capturing the perfect photo, breathing in the fresh mountain air, or simply taking in the peaceful beauty around you, Belmont Lookout is a place that stays with you long after you’ve left. It's the perfect stop for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and anyone wanting to see St. Vincent from a whole new perspective. Don’t forget your camera — this view is unforgettable!

Black Point Tunnel

Black Point Tunnel, also known as Jasper Rock Tunnel, is a historic site located on the eastern coast of Saint Vincent, near Byera Hill. Constructed between 1812 and 1815 by the British using enslaved African labor, the approximately 300-foot-long tunnel was designed to facilitate the transportation of sugar from inland plantations to coastal ships for export. This engineering feat involved manually cutting through volcanic rock using basic tools. The tunnel is part of the Black Point Heritage and Recreational Park, which offers visitors amenities such as gazebos, washrooms, and barbecue pits. Notably, the site served as a filming location for the movie "Pirates of the Caribbean: Curse of the Black Pearl." Located approximately 20 miles (32 kilometers) from Kingstown, reaching Black Point Tunnel by taxi typically takes about an hour, depending on traffic.

Montreal Garden

Discover the Hidden Gem ofMontreal Gardens (St Vincent) Located in the mountains above the Mesopotamia Valley, the Montreal Gardens estate is blessed with fertile volcanic soil and frequent rainfall. You will find an array of exotic flowers, animals and plants in an environment which is cool, misty and quiet. The Gardens are open to the public for a small entrance fee

River14

Nestled in the South Rivers community within the Colonarie River basin, River 14 is a serene recreational site that has evolved from a traditional communal space into a cherished eco-tourism destination. Historically used by locals for daily activities like bathing and washing, the area now offers visitors a tranquil environment surrounded by lush greenery and the gentle flow of the river. It's an ideal spot for picnics, leisurely walks, and immersing oneself in nature's beauty. The site has been thoughtfully developed to enhance its natural charm while providing basic amenities for a comfortable visit.
