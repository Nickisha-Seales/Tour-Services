Explore the Heart of St. Vincent

Begin your journey with a visit to the beautiful Botanical Gardens the oldest in the Western Hemisphere, filled with exotic plants and colorful birds. Next, step back in time at Fort Charlotte, perched high above the coastline with stunning panoramic views. Continue to Fort Duvernette, a historic fort built on a rocky islet a short boat ride takes you to this unique landmark. After exploring, relax at Young Island Resort, a private island getaway. Enjoy a delicious lunch, then spend the rest of the afternoon sunbathing on the beach or snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters. A perfect mix of history, beauty, and relaxation!

Botanical Garden

Botanical Garden (St Vincent) Located on 20 acres of land on the outskirts of the capital Kingstown, the Botanic Gardens are the oldest of their kind in the Western Hemisphere. Among the wide variety of tropical trees and shrubs is a breadfruit tree from the original plant brought to the country by Captain Bligh (of The Bounty fame) in 1793. Conservation of rare species of plants has been practised since the Gardens were founded in 1765. Other conservation works involve the national bird, the St Vincent Parrot (Amazona Guildingii). Facilities offered here are washrooms, a small performance area, gazebos, refreshment and craft sales. The Gardens are a popular spot for wedding ceremonies.

Fort Charlotte

Fort Charlotte Fort Charlotte, located just west of Kingstown, Saint Vincent, is a historic British-colonial era fort built between 1763 and 1806. Perched approximately 600 feet above sea level, it was originally constructed to defend against French attacks, Carib unrest, and slave rebellions. The fort could accommodate around 600 troops and was equipped with over 30 cannons, many of which were strategically pointed inland to guard against land-based threats. Today, visitors can explore the remaining structures and enjoy panoramic views of Kingstown, the Grenadines, and the surrounding Caribbean Sea.

Fort Duvernette

Discover the Hidden Gem of Fort Duvernette For a truly unforgettable adventure in St. Vincent, set your sights on Fort Duvernette, a historic fortress dramatically perched atop a towering 195-foot volcanic rock just off the southern coast. Accessible only by a short boat ride from the Young Island dock, this one-of-a-kind site offers both history and breathtaking scenery. Climb the 255 stone steps to the summit and be rewarded with panoramic views of the Caribbean Sea, Young Island, and the surrounding coastline. Originally built in the 1790s to protect the nearby Calliaqua Bay, Fort Duvernette still holds remnants of its old cannons and colonial charm. Whether you're a history enthusiast, a nature lover, or just looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience, Fort Duvernette is a must-visit treasure that perfectly blends culture, adventure, and stunning views.
